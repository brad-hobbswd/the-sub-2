Many conversations about addiction focus on substances, behaviors, consequences, and recovery, yet far fewer explore the deeper realities that often exist beneath the struggle. Questions about emotional wounds, hidden fears, loneliness, identity, shame, and the search for relief are frequently overshadowed by the visible effects of addiction, leaving much of the story untold.

The Substitute explores a question that reaches beyond addiction itself and into the heart of the person who needs the substitute. Rather than focusing solely on destructive behaviors, this book examines the emotional, relational, and spiritual realities that often shape those behaviors while inviting readers to look beneath the surface and discover the deeper story that exists within every struggle.

Through discussions of pain, shame, fear, loneliness, recovery, healing, and faith, readers are guided through a journey that seeks not only to understand addiction more deeply, but also to understand the human heart and the needs, longings, and wounds that often remain hidden beneath the struggle. Along the way, a greater picture begins to emerge, revealing that behind every addiction is a person, behind every person is a story, and within every story is a heart searching for peace, belonging, purpose, and hope.

About This Book

The first volume of The Substitute asked a simple yet powerful question: What is the addiction substituting for? This second volume explores a different question: What is happening inside the heart of the person who needs the substitute? Throughout these chapters, readers journey beneath the surface of addiction and into the realities that often shape the struggle. Emotional wounds, shame, fear, loneliness, identity, recovery, healing, and spiritual longing are examined through the lens of compassion, understanding, and hope. Rather than focusing solely on addiction itself, this book focuses on the human heart and the deeper needs that often exist beneath destructive behaviors.

Inside This Journey

This journey begins by looking beyond addiction itself and focusing on the person behind the struggle. Along the way, readers will explore emotional wounds, hidden pain, shame, fear, and loneliness while gaining a deeper understanding of the experiences that often shape destructive behaviors and the search for relief.

As the journey continues, attention turns toward identity, recovery, and healing, revealing how the stories people believe about themselves can influence their choices, relationships, and sense of hope for the future. Through honest reflection and compassionate understanding, readers are invited to discover that lasting change involves far more than removing a substitute. Lasting change often begins when the deeper needs of the heart receive the attention they deserve.

The final chapters explore healing, purpose, faith, and the hope found in Jesus, bringing together the themes of the entire book while pointing toward the possibility of restoration, freedom, and a deeper understanding of what the human heart has been searching for all along.

About the Author

Bradley Hobbs writes about healing, recovery, faith, emotional growth, identity, and the deeper questions that shape the human experience. Drawing from personal experience, recovery communities, spiritual reflection, and a passion for helping others heal, his work seeks to bring understanding, encouragement, compassion, and hope to readers navigating life's most difficult struggles.

The Substitute continues the journey begun in What Is the Addiction Substituting For?, expanding the conversation beyond addiction itself and into the deeper realities of the human heart.
